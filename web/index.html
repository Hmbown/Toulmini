The Process

Data. Warrant. Rebuttal. Verdict.

1

Ground

DATA + CLAIM

Extract verifiable facts with citations. Construct a falsifiable claim. No hedging allowed.

2

Bridge

WARRANT + BACKING

Build the logical connection. Provide authoritative support. Weak backing = chain terminates.

3

Attack

REBUTTAL + QUALIFIER

Adversarial stress test. Find every weakness. Assign honest certainty levels.

4

Judge

VERDICT

Final judgment. Sustained, overruled, or remanded. No appeals.

The Argumentation Model

Stephen Edelston Toulmin (1922–2009) was a British philosopher, author, and educator. His 1958 work "The Uses of Argument" introduced a model of practical reasoning that challenged the dominance of formal logic in philosophy.

Toulmin argued that most real-world arguments don't follow the neat patterns of syllogistic logic. Instead, they involve claims supported by data, connected through warrants, backed by authority, qualified by degree of certainty, and vulnerable to rebuttals.

Toulmini applies this model to force LLMs into rigorous, structured reasoning—making uncertainty explicit rather than hidden behind hedging language.

"Logic is not concerned with how we think, but with how we ought to think if we want to think correctly."
